## Wasp Nest | Manifesto

https://web.archive.org/web/20231128132520/https://punkwasp.leprd.space/manifesto.php

\[Think about people with disability\]

Purposely choosing color combinations with such poor contrast that even people with good vision can't quite make out the words, having rapidly flashing GIFs all over your site and no warnings or way to turn them off, and using pixels instead of responsive options to set font sizes so that a person with low vision can't adjust the font size in their browser - these are choices that make the indie web at best difficult to navigate and at worst actively dangerous to navigate for disabled people.

More people have phones or mobile devices than PCs or laptops, particularly poor people and disabled people (who often use them for accessibility reasons). I strive to ensure my website is responsive and looks at least somewhat okay on every device
